Salmon Sushi

This recipe features fresh salmon, avocado, and cucumber wrapped up in a nori sheet and seasoned with soy sauce and wasabi. It's perfect for a light lunch or dinner, and is sure to satisfy your cravings for something savory and satisfying.

    Ingredients needed:
  • 1 cup sushi rice
  • 1 1/4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 pound fresh salmon, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 4 sheets nori
  • Soy sauce, for serving
  • Wasabi, for serving

Steps:

  1. 1 Rinse the sushi rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Combine the rice and water in a medium saucepan and bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Cook for 18 minutes, then remove from heat and let stand for 10 minutes.
  2. 2 In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium heat until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat and let cool.
  3. 3 Spread the cooked rice over a large sheet of parchment paper, then sprinkle the vinegar mixture over the rice. Use a wooden spoon to mix the rice and vinegar together.
  4. 4 Lay a sheet of nori on a flat surface, with the shiny side facing down. Wet your hands with water, then spread a thin layer of rice over the nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top of the sheet.
  5. 5 Lay a few strips of salmon, avocado, and cucumber over the rice, near the bottom edge of the sheet. Use your fingers to roll the edge of the nori up and over the filling, then continue rolling until the sushi is completely wrapped up.
  6. 6 Repeat with the remaining nori sheets and filling ingredients.
  7. 7 Serve the sushi with soy sauce and wasabi on the side. Enjoy!

    8. Note: This recipe makes 4 rolls of sushi.
